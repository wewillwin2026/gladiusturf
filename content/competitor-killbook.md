## Executive summary

The landscape software market is **structurally weak in three identical places**: (1) the mobile/field experience is universally bad — every vendor has an app that crashes, drains battery, or fails offline; (2) AI is bolted on as marketing, not native — none of them ship a system that *thinks*, they ship CRMs that store; (3) pricing is opaque or punitive — per-user/per-tech math punishes growing crews and lock-in contracts trap shops who try to leave.

**Most dangerous to GladiusTurf:** Aspire (ServiceTitan-owned, owns the $2M+ enterprise tier, deepest pockets) > LMN (most loved by mid-market, strongest brand in landscape-native) > ServiceTitan/FieldRoutes (will eventually attack adjacent verticals with category capital).

**Easiest kills:** Arborgold (3.1/5 Capterra, deliverability broken, niche and bleeding), HindSite (irrigation legacy, can't take payment in-app), CLIPitc (forced cloud migration alienated their base), Real Green (BBB complaints, locked-in payment processor), Method:CRM (built for QuickBooks contractors not landscape ops).

**The shared blindspot every competitor has:** **They are systems of record, not systems of revenue.** Nobody ships "what's the next dollar" — they ship "where's the data." None of them have Quote Intercept, Ghost Recovery, Upsell Whisperer, Referral Radar, or Operator Score. They digitize landscape work. **GladiusTurf compounds landscape revenue.** That is the category we are creating.

**Attack order:** P0 = LMN (largest landscape-native install base, mid-market $397–$997 sweet spot); P0 = Jobber (widest install base, weakest moat, switching cost ~$0); P1 = Aspire (positioning, not migration — write the manifesto that kills it); P1 = Service Autopilot (per-user pricing creates natural defectors); P2 = the rest.

## The category move

**GladiusTurf is not competing in "landscape software." We are creating Landscape Revenue Intelligence (LRI).** Aspire/LMN/SingleOps fight over "who has the best estimating module" and "who has the cleanest scheduler." That's last category. The new category is: *which platform compounds revenue per crew per season?*

The proof points are baked into the product (LRI Score, Operator Score, Outcome Graph from sister-product Gladius CRM). The category vocabulary is in the brand:
- **"Quote Intercept"** (you're losing 1/3 of pipeline to silence — we won't let you)
- **"Ghost Recovery"** (50% of dead leads buy in 90 days — we catch them)
- **"Site Memory"** (the gate code, the dog, the sprinkler — every crew handoff carries 100 facts)
- **"Operator Score"** (portable crew reputation that survives employer change)
- **"Surplus Yard"** (leftover sod becomes revenue, not waste)

1. **Manifesto post (Day 7):** "Landscape software is a system of record. Landscape revenue intelligence is a system of revenue. Here's the difference, in 27 engines." Publish on the landing page. Cross-post LinkedIn, LawnSite, r/lawncare, r/landscaping, GIE+EXPO Slack groups.
