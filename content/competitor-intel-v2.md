### 1. Aspire Software (ServiceTitan) — 2026 deep file

**Real pricing.** Quote-only. Aspire publicly states pricing is "based on company size, complexity, and what solution best fits" — annual term, price-protected for term, monthly billing, unlimited users included. No published floor; LawnSite contractor disclosures put real-world minimums in the **low four-figures/month** range with **102% YoY hike on a single line item documented**. Cancellation language: "If a customer terminates the Aspire agreement for any reason other than for cause, the customer shall pay a separation fee equal to the subscription fees for the remainder of the then-current term."

**Recent churn quotes (2024–2026)**:
1. *"I highly do not recommend Aspire. If you are going to use Aspire the only way I can see you getting by with it is if you only do maintenance and offer no other services. If you do design build, then I could go on and on as to why Aspire is not suited for design build companies."* — LawnSite (https://www.lawnsite.com/threads/aspire-thoughts.502607/)
2. *"Visually dense and hard to navigate. Even experienced Aspire customers occasionally need assistance using the software."* — Capterra (https://www.capterra.com/p/161544/Aspire/reviews/)
3. *"Steep learning curve and limited support responsiveness."* — Software Advice (https://www.softwareadvice.com/field-service/aspire-profile/reviews/)
4. *"Aspire Billing is very manipulative and predatory… overcharged us and took 18 months to fix it."* — credit issued only as future-product credit, not refund. LawnSite.
5. *"102% increase in just one line item."* — LawnSite, contractor renewal disclosure.

**Roadmap signals (last 6mo).** Aspire's parent ServiceTitan is **hiring an "AI Automation Engineer"** (PitchMeAI, 2025) to "design, build, and ship AI agents" embedded inside portfolio companies — meaning Aspire's AI is a 2026 hire, not a 2024 product. Shipped in 2024–25: offline mobile, AI property measurement (PropertyIntel), AI User Guide (search-the-help-docs), expanded construction module (Gantt charts, equipment scheduling). What they have NOT shipped: native quote-recovery AI, ghost-lead reactivation, native AI cadence editor, real-time tone analysis on customer calls. Sources: [PitchMeAI Aspire AI Engineer JD](https://pitchmeai.com/jobs/aspire-software/ai-automation-engineer-s6zend05mt), [Aspire AI webinar recap](https://www.youraspire.com/blog/webinar-recap-unlocking-hyper-efficiency), [Aspire construction press](https://www.greenindustrypros.com/business-management/software/product/22921670/aspire-software-aspire-software-unveils-new-suite-of-features), [Aspire ToS](https://www.youraspire.com/legal/terms-of-service)

## The shared 5 weaknesses every Tier-1 competitor has

1. **AI is OEM'd, gated, or vaporware.** Aspire hires AI engineers in 2025. LMN OEMs Attentive.ai. Service Autopilot's "Genius AI" missing from 2025 release notes. Jobber gates AI Receptionist behind $99/mo add-on. RealGreen ships SMS notifications and calls it modern. SingleOps ships "send-reminder-on-overdue" automation. Arborgold ships SMS in November 2025 like it's news. HindSite, CLIPitc — silent. **Every single Tier-1 competitor has either no first-party AI, AI behind a paywall, or AI that lives in a press release.**

2. **The mobile/field app is uniformly broken.** LMN Crew app deletes job cards (2.7★). RealGreen mobile requires deletion-and-reinstall. SingleOps mobile crashes and drains battery. Arborgold mobile 2.8★. Aspire mobile rated "visually dense and hard to navigate." HindSite can't take payment in the field at all. CLIPitc is a desktop app dragged to web. **No competitor ships an offline-first PWA that actually works in dead zones.**

3. **Per-user / per-revenue pricing punishes growth.** Jobber +$29/seat. SingleOps +$55–$150/seat by tier. HindSite +$20/seat. Aspire revenue-tied with 102% YoY hikes documented. RealGreen card-on-file year contracts. Service Autopilot tiered pricing "removed without notice." **The harder you grow, the more they charge.**

4. **Cancellation is a trap.** Aspire's separation fee = remaining subscription. RealGreen's collections threats. LMN's 30-day cancellation usage fee. SingleOps' "couldn't cancel until 2026" auto-renewal. Service Autopilot's "tried for 3 months while still being charged." **Every Tier-1 has either contractual or operational anti-cancellation friction.**

5. **They are systems of record, not systems of revenue.** Every competitor describes itself with verbs that store: "manage, schedule, route, invoice, track." None describe themselves with verbs that compound: "intercept, recover, upsell, refer, predict." This is the category gap — and the category we own with Quote Intercept, Ghost Recovery, Upsell Whisperer, Referral Radar, Operator Score, Site Memory, ToneRadar.
